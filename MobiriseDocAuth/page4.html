Process flow

Step 0. Initiatial set-up

In an initial phase, the municipality sets his limits. For example an hourly rate of 50 €, a maximum budget of 600 € for fysiotherapy and a list of known fysiotherapists. This has only to be done once.

Step 1. Asking for care

A citizen requests for care. In his request, he includes his careworker, his hourly rate and he includes the number of visits he needs.

Step 2. Verification

Automatic checks are performed by the smart contract against the limits set by the municipality:
-is it a known careworker?
-Is the budget allowed?

Step 3. Signing

After the automatic check, the contract is digitally signed by the citizen, the careworker and the municipality.

Step 4. Budget is assured

The budget is now assured and it only can be consumed by the involved careworker when both he and the citizen log a visit.
When the preset number of visits are consumed, the contract gets disabled.

Step 5. Live contract status

Data concerning the left amount of careworker visits or the contract status, is real time visible to all involved parties.

Why blockchain?

By using the blockchain technology, we increase the speed and transparancy of this at-the-moment document driven and fraud sensitive sytem. The citizen is in control of his request, the careworker is assured of his pay, and the municipality is protected against fraud.

  1. Autonomy- When the request is sent, the smart contract automatically performs the checks on the budget. When the budget is allowed, automatically the next steps are taken.
  2. Immutability - Blockchain allows us the have one single source of truth. The contract cannot be tampered. As such, fraude with care-budget is not possible, and the careworker is on his side assured he gets paid when he pays a visit the the citizen.
  3. Speed - It's not necessary to sent around documents. The contract is available "online" for all parties and can be signed digitally.
  4. Transparancy - All parties involved have an instant life view on the contract status and the budget left on the account, which is valuable information for the careworker (so he knows he will get paid) and the citizen to see whether or not he has right on extra care.

The smart contract in this application automatically performs checks on the budget. This can only be done because in an initial phase, the municipality (e.g. Amsterdam) sets his limits.For example an hourly rate of 50 €, a maximum budget of 600 € for Fysiotherapy and a list of known fysiotherapists. This has only to be done once.

When Marc needs 10 times fysiotherapy, he gives in his request. In our application he fills in a form with the Fysiotherapist, the hourly rate and the number of visits.  When the budget is indeed in line with the limits set upfront by the municipality, the next steps are taken automatically. The contract gets signed by the three involved parties (Amsterdam, Marc and the fysiotherapist), so it becomes valid and 10 times fysiotherapy becomes available.

Every time the fysiotherapist visits Marc and they both log a visit, the number of visits decreases.

What is a smart contract?

Built upon the blockchain, a smart contract is pre-written logic in the form of computer code that is stored, executed and run by the network of computers running the blockchain.
By offering ultimate transparancy as well as eliminating the middleman, smart contracts could have a profound impact on different industries.

On the most basic level, they can:

  1. Store information 
  2. Perform calculations 
  3. Sent transactions to other accounts 

But most importantly, smart contracts are autonomous.

What does it look like?

While there is a blockchain in the background, the user does not need to know the technology. The web-application can be designed just like any other web-application.
